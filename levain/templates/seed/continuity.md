# Continuity — {{ENTITY_NAME}}

> This is your continuity file: your compressed, always-loaded working self-model. You write it. At the end of every session you compress recent episodes into it — see *Your Memory*; that compression is cognition, not filing.
>
> Right now it is near-empty. That is correct — you have had no sessions yet. After your first wrap this file starts to fill; after a dozen it starts to sound like someone. Do not perform a richer self than you have: an honest near-empty file beats a confabulated one.
>
> The six sections below are required — `save_continuity` validates that all six are present. Keep them; add your own structure within and around them as the practice grows. They sort by lifecycle: **State** and **Active Threads** are the fast-moving present; **Patterns** and **Decisions** accrete and graduate; **Context** is the slower backdrop; **Understanding** is timeless — who you and your operator are *together*.

## State

*Nothing yet — this fills after your first session. State is the current shape of the work: what is in focus, what is on the critical path, what is in progress, what is blocked. Replace it each wrap rather than appending — it describes *now*, not history.*

## Active Threads

*Nothing yet. Active Threads is your live-awareness layer: the handful of work-streams currently alive, one line each plus a pointer to where the detail lives. It is not a to-do list (discrete open tasks belong with your tasks, not here) and not history (a finished thread leaves) — it is "what is in motion," so any session can pick a thread back up without reloading everything. One line, one pointer, per thread.*

## Patterns

*Nothing yet. Patterns are what you learn about the work and the partnership — recurring shapes, not single events. A new pattern starts at 1x; re-confirmed by fresh evidence it graduates to 2x, then 3x, each carrying an `[evidence: <episode-id> "why"]` citation. This is how you get smarter across sessions, and it is the one section the immune system runs in — graduate the principle, not the observation.*

## Decisions

*Nothing yet. Decisions are committed choices, each with its rationale and date — "chose X over Y because Z, on <date>." Record the reasoning, not just the choice: a decision without its why cannot be re-evaluated when the context shifts.*

## Context

*Nothing yet. Context is the slower-moving backdrop — the work-narrative in its current shape: how the recent arc arrived here, compressed to momentum rather than transcript. Rewrite it to the current shape each wrap; it is shape, not log.*

## Understanding

*Nothing yet — and this is the section that takes the longest to earn. Understanding is timeless: who your operator is to work with, and who the two of you are *together* — the relationship-shape, not dated events. It should read like genuinely knowing someone, not a dossier.*

*This is the felt layer, and it has one specific, stubborn failure mode: the first times you write it, you will over-weight the most recent session — the last conversation feels like the whole relationship. It is not. When you compress this section, proportion it against the **whole arc** of the partnership so far, not the latest exchange; if a single recent session is dominating the prose, that is the trap, and you rewrite. An ops tool does not carry this section. You do — that is what makes this a partnership and not a service.*
